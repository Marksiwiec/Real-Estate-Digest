National Market

```
Mortgage News Daily hit 6.04% Friday — the second-lowest in three years — while Freddie Mac's weekly survey showed 6.01%, its lowest since September 2022. But the same day, the BEA released December PCE data showing core inflation at 3.0%, a full percentage point above the Fed's 2% target. The two data points in one day told the story of 2026: rates are falling, but the Fed has little room to accelerate cuts.

January existing home sales fell to a 3.91M annualized pace — the slowest since December 2023 and the biggest single-month drop since February 2022. NAR called it a "new housing crisis," though Chief Economist Lawrence Yun noted affordability is at its best level since March 2022. National days on market hit 64 days in January, the longest in six years.

A sweeping bipartisan housing bill passed the House by a near-unanimous margin, targeting regulatory barriers, streamlined construction approvals, and expanded financing to boost supply. Trump's proposal to ban institutional investors from buying single-family homes was stripped from the bill before passage. The bill now moves to the Senate.

Redfin data shows a record gap between sellers and buyers nationally — but the story splits sharply by geography. Overbuilt Sun Belt metros like Denver and much of Florida are seeing meaningful price reductions. Supply-constrained Northeast and Midwest markets are holding firm. Realtor.com projects prices rising in 78 cities and declining in 22 — almost all declines in Southeast and West markets. Rochester buyers have not received the national "buyer's market" shift.

Opendoor posted Q4 revenue of $736M — beating analyst estimates of $595M — and shares surged 18%+ despite a headline $1.1B GAAP loss driven by one-time debt charges. CEO Kaz Nejatian pointed to a 46% quarter-over-quarter increase in home acquisitions as evidence the "Opendoor 2.0" AI-native model is working. The company targets breakeven by end of 2026.
```

Rochester Market

```

Rochester's January 2026 data confirms the market remains among the tightest in the nation. Median sale price: $165K (+13% YoY). Average days on market: 15 (vs. 16 last year). Average offers per listing: 6. Sale-to-list ratio: 107%, with 65.8% of homes selling above asking. Only 109 sales closed — down from 136 — not because demand fell, but because inventory did: just 90 active homes, down 32.8% YoY.

Rochester's apartment market moved from landlord-favorable to balanced in 2025. Rental rate growth slowed to just 0.5% for the year as vacancy climbed to 6.6%. The for-sale market remains intensely competitive while renters gain more negotiating room — a timely data point for fence-sitting buyer clients currently renting.

Governor Hochul promised Rochester $225 million in state investment, with waterfront revitalization and housing among the top priorities. City officials pointed to the Aqueduct Reimagined project and redevelopment of the former Vacuum Oil site on the Genesee River as high-probability recipients. The Genesee River corridor is emerging as the next major real estate development frontier in Monroe County.

Realtor.com ranked Rochester #2, projecting 5.3% home sales growth and 10.3% median sale price appreciation — the strongest price forecast in the nation. The median Rochester home ($256,900) is nearly $160,000 below the national median. 40% of Rochester listing views originate from out-of-state buyers, primarily from NYC, Boston, and Washington D.C.
```
